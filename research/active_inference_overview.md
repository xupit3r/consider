# Active Inference Overview

Active Inference is a mathematical framework that explains how biological and artificial systems learn, perceive, and act by minimizing prediction errors. It is a unified framework for cognitive modeling that integrates perception, action, and learning.

## Key Principles

- **Free Energy Principle (FEP)**: The mathematical foundation for self-organizing systems. It states that systems maintain their integrity by minimizing variational free energy.
- **Predictive Processing**: Hierarchical prediction and error minimization. The system constantly generates predictions about sensory input and updates its internal state based on the mismatch (prediction error).
- **Perceptual Inference**: Changing internal beliefs to better match sensory data.
- **Active Inference (Action)**: Changing the world through action to make sensory data match internal predictions.

## Core Components

- **Generative Model**: An internal probabilistic representation of the environment.
- **Markov Blankets**: A boundary that separates internal states from external states, defining the agent's interface with the world.
- **Policy Selection**: The process of choosing actions that minimize expected free energy in the future.

## Applications

- Cognitive architectures and agent design
- Neural implementation and brain modeling
- Social cognition and multi-agent coordination
- Robotics and autonomous systems
- Healthcare, finance, and environmental management
